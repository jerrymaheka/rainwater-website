What It Is

Introduction to Rainwater Harvesting

Rainwater harvesting is the process of collecting and storing rainwater from rooftops, land surfaces, and other catchment areas for later use. Instead of letting precipitation run off into drains, this practice channels it into tanks, cisterns, or underground reservoirs.

Used for thousands of years across Africa, Asia, and beyond, rainwater harvesting is one of humanity's oldest and most practical responses to water scarcity — and it is more relevant today than ever before.

Take Action

Water Conservation Tips

Small habits add up. Here are four practical steps anyone can take to conserve water every day.

🔧

Fix Leaking Taps

A dripping tap can waste thousands of litres per year. Repair it promptly and save both water and money.

🪣

Buckets Over Hoses

A garden hose can use up to 1,000 litres per hour. Switch to a bucket for washing and watering tasks.

🌧️

Collect Rainwater

Place tanks or barrels under downpipes to capture roof runoff and irrigate your garden for free.

♻️

Reuse Greywater

Water from sinks and showers can safely be redirected to flush toilets or water non-edible plants.

Why It Matters

Benefits of Rainwater Harvesting

From household savings to community resilience, the advantages of harvesting rainwater reach far and wide.

Saves water by reducing dependence on municipal supply
Reduces water bills for households and businesses
Provides a reliable backup supply during droughts
Supports healthy gardening with naturally soft water
